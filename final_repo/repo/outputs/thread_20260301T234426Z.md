**Query:** “What arguments are made for Universal Basic Income as a response to AI-driven automation?”

## Answer

According to the provided context, the following arguments are made for Universal Basic Income as a response to AI-driven automation:

1. Addressing Technological Unemployment: The evidence from sources like Subaveerapandiyan & Shimray (2024), Georgieff & Hyee (2022), and Wang & Lu (2025) indicates that UBI can address technological unemployment caused by AI usage, which is both a current and future driver of economic fragility. (tan_2025, tan_2025_chunk_034)
2. Universal Basic Income as a stabilising mechanism: The examination of UBI in Section 4.3 is motivated by the need to buffer individuals from the negative impacts of AI-driven automation on the labour market, which threatens to create a divided labour market between those who can collaborate with AI effectively and those structurally excluded. (park_2025, park_2025_chunk_110)
3. UBI as a comprehensive solution: The paper argues that UBI demonstrates considerable promise in alleviating poverty caused by technological unemployment, making it a potential comprehensive solution to the challenges posed by such automation. (tan_2025, tan_2025_chunk_001)
