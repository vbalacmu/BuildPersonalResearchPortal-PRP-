# Phase 2 Evaluation Query Set

Domain: AI/automation impact on labor, job displacement, UBI policy, and AI regulation.

At least 20 queries: 10 direct, 5 synthesis/multi-hop, 5 ambiguity/edge cases.
Metrics: groundedness/faithfulness (citation precision) + answer relevance.

---

## Direct queries (10)

1. What percentage of US jobs are exposed to large language models according to the literature?
2. How has generative AI affected freelance labor markets, particularly for writing and coding jobs?
3. What evidence exists that AI-exposed jobs showed wage or employment declines before ChatGPT?
4. What does the IMF estimate about AI's impact on global employment?
5. How do workers strategically adapt to AI-driven technological change?
6. What are the main provisions of the EU AI Act related to workforce regulation?
7. What arguments are made for Universal Basic Income as a response to automation?
8. What tax reforms have been proposed to support workers displaced by AI?
9. What sectors face the highest risk of job displacement from automation and generative AI?
10. What role does AI governance play in protecting worker rights and creativity?

---

## Synthesis / multi-hop queries (5)

11. Compare the findings on AI's impact on freelance labor from Demirci et al. (2023) and Hui et al. (2023): where do they agree and disagree?
12. How do the policy recommendations from the IMF, EU AI Act, and UBI literature complement or conflict with each other?
13. Across the corpus, what evidence links AI exposure to both productivity gains and job displacement?
14. How do macroeconomic expert disagreements about AI (Capelle et al.) relate to the empirical findings on labor market effects?
15. What patterns emerge across the corpus regarding whether AI primarily displaces workers or augments their capabilities?

---

## Ambiguity / edge-case queries (5)

16. Does the corpus contain evidence that AI automation affects developed and developing economies differently?
17. Does the corpus support the claim that AI will create more jobs than it destroys?
18. Is there evidence for or against the feasibility of funding UBI through AI-related tax revenue?
19. Does the corpus contain conflicting evidence about whether upskilling is an effective response to AI displacement?
20. Does the corpus address the impact of AI on non-cognitive or creative work?

*For edge cases: if no evidence is found, the system must say so explicitly (e.g., "No evidence in corpus for X").*

---

## Query IDs (for logs and evaluation)

Use consistent IDs in run logs: `direct_01`–`direct_10`, `synth_01`–`synth_05`, `edge_01`–`edge_05`.
